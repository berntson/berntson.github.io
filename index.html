Papers

  • Against Modus Ponens for Might forthcoming in Analysis

    Suppose that you and I are at the mint and see a new coin. We have no way of knowing whether the coin will ever be flipped. Still, we know something: we know that if the coin were flipped, it might land heads, and that if it were flipped, it might land tails. Surprisingly, this simple case is flatly inconsistent with theories of counterfactuals from Lewis and Stalnaker. In this short paper I explain why, and consider how the problem might be solved.

  • Nominalism and Immutability

    Can we do science without numbers? Hartry Field showed how, but his strategy requires the existence of spacetime points. I argue for an alternative. The key is immutabilism, the view that there is contingency about what exists, but none about how things are. Although this metaphysical thesis seems unrelated to nominalism, it yields a new strategy for doing science without numbers, and one that does not require spacetime points.

  • Near Similarity and Counterfactuals

    David Lewis analyzes counterfactuals in terms of maximal similarity. In this paper, I argue that they should instead be analyzed in terms of near similarity. After giving two arguments for this shift, I develop near-similarity semantics as a positive proposal by providing models and a corresponding proof theory. A striking feature of the resulting semantics is that the similarity relation it employs need not be transitive. As a result, it invalidates the inference rule Strengthen Might.

  • The Counterfactual Logic of Interval Orders and Semiorders

    Counterfactuals are often modeled using comparative relations on possible worlds. Such relations include total orders (Stalnaker), total preorders (Lewis), and preorders (Pollock, Kratzer). In this paper, I extend this research program by introducing new systems of counterfactual logic based on interval orders and semiorders. I then show that all the new systems are weakly complete and decidable, and that many are strongly complete, and therefore also compact.
