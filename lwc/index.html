Welcome

Be still a while.

The Little White Church stands on Clark Street in Paullina, a few blocks from the schoolhouse. It was built when Scandinavian families here needed somewhere to sing, to baptize, and to bear with one another. The pews have heard a long century of hymns — in Norwegian, in Swedish, and in English.

It is now kept by the Berntson family, who open the doors for hymn sings, for the local spinners and their wheels, for homeschoolers down the road, and for any friend who wants a still room for an afternoon. The doors aren't always open — but send word, and we'll be glad to meet you there.

Our Story

A church on Clark Street.

Late 1800s

Founded as Siloam Lutheran

Scandinavian settlers in this corner of O'Brien County — families with names like Axdahl, Bjork, Stangeland, and Larson — built Siloam Lutheran a few blocks from the schoolhouse, on what is now Clark Street. The little white-clapboard building, with its square bell tower, became a steady landmark in town.

A long century

Norwegian, Swedish, English

Generations of families filled these pews. The hymnal opened to Built on the Rock, to Children of the Heavenly Father — first in Norwegian and Swedish, and later in English. Children were baptized in the same font their grandparents had been baptized in. Pastors came and went — P. L. Kvitne, Stanley Hansen — and the building weathered Iowa winters and summer storms alike.

[year]

A new chapter

In time the congregation closed its doors. The Berntson family acquired the building, intent on keeping it. Today it is known simply as The Little White Church — a quietly held place for memory, song, handwork, and the slow company of friends.
