Welcome

Come as you are.

The Little White Church sits at the edge of a quiet country road, where prairie wind moves through the grass and the sky goes on for miles. Whether you grew up here, are passing through, or are looking for a peaceful place to gather, we're glad you've found us.

The church is lovingly kept by the Berntson family, who continue a tradition of care that began with the original congregation more than a century ago. The doors aren't always open, but visitors are always welcome — just send us a note ahead of time.

Our Story

A church on the prairie.

Late 1800s

Founded as Siloam Lutheran

Scandinavian settlers in O'Brien County — families with names like Axdahl, Bjork, Stangeland, and Larson — built Siloam Lutheran as a gathering place for worship, weddings, and Sunday school. The little white-clapboard building, with its modest steeple, became a landmark on the prairie.

A long century

Norwegian, Swedish, English

Generations of families filled its pews. Hymns were sung first in Norwegian and Swedish, and later in English. Children were baptized in the same font their grandparents had been baptized in. Pastors came and went — among them P. L. Kvitne and Stanley Hansen — and the building weathered Iowa winters and prairie storms alike.

[year]

A new chapter

In time the congregation closed its doors. The Berntson family acquired the building soon after, intent on preserving it. Today it's known simply as The Little White Church, a quietly kept place for memory, gathering, and the occasional service.

Have a memory of Siloam Lutheran? We'd love to hear it — see Visit.
